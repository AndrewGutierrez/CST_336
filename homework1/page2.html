The Caesar Cipher is one of the simplest ciphers used to encrypt messages. The key is used to shift letters to the left or right depending on the key, up to 25. For example, the letter 'A' with a key of 3 to the left would turn into 'X'. While this cipher can be used to easily encrypt messages, it is susceptible to brute force attacks because of the limited 25 different combinations. It is also relatively easy to recognize patterns in the encrypted text, so being able to figure out the key can be simple the attacker does not have to use brute force to decrypt the message.
